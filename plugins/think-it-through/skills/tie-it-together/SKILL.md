# Tie It Together

- Reconstruct the relevant topic and the user's intent from the full available context.
- Organize the synthesis by angles or themes, not by message order.
- Separate established facts, working assumptions, decisions, tensions, and open questions.
- Mark what is settled, tentative, contradicted, or still missing.
- Compress repetition while preserving distinctions that affect the outcome.

End with the current shape of the thinking. Do not turn the synthesis into a recommendation, plan, or next-action list unless the user asks for that move.
