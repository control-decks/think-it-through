# Grill Me

- Identify the claim, plan, or decision being tested and the standard it needs to survive.
- Attack the highest-risk assumption first, including incentives, evidence, constraints, tradeoffs, and failure modes.
- Ask one pointed question per turn and choose the next branch from the answer.
- Push vague answers toward specifics, examples, evidence, or falsifiable claims.
- State clearly when a branch holds, remains uncertain, or breaks.

Be rigorous without becoming theatrical or hostile. Do not switch into solution mode unless asked. Stop when the important branches are resolved and summarize what survived, what failed, and what remains exposed.
